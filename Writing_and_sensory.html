Sensory Bins

The point of the sensory bin is to play with objects of different textures. To make a sensory bin, crush everyday objects like cheerios and graham crackers to create a substance that resembles sand, which is collected into a bin. The child can play in this "sand" bin. Other things you can include in the sensory bin are dry pasta, beans, rice, cooked pasta, and mashed potatoes. When all of this is inside the bin, you can add toys into the bin as well, and have your child hunt for the "treasure." Your child could write letters inside the sensory bins, tracing letters in the "sand" or other objects. For an additional sensory element, you can have your child clean the toys found in the sensory bins using a sponge with warm water and soap.

Food

The point of this activity is to use food to make letters and shapes. Food like cheerios, croutons, chocolate chips, cut-up vegetables, thin pretzels rods, and marshmallows can be used to teach your child how to write. With young kids, you would trace a shape on paper, like a plus sign, circle, square, or triangle, and have them place the food on top of that shape. With older kids, you would do the same with letters. With less solid foods like whipped cream or mashed potatoes, you could have your child use his or her fingers to trace the shape or letters through them. Another activity can be to form shapes out of toothpicks and marshmallows, as shown in the picture. The marshmallow holds the toothpicks together and so one can form many shapes with them. An oral sensory activity that incorporates food is to have your child taste different foods of different textures and temperatures.

Movement

The point of this activity is to use your whole body to learn the skill of writing. The example shown in the picture depicts using arms or scarves to write letters in the air. One can also use painter's tape to form shapes or letters on the floor, and then have the child jump, run, or walk on the letter or shape. Additionally, one can put the painter's tape on the wall in shapes or letters and have the child trace them on the wall or hit a ball on the letter or shape you select. This is a fun way to get kids to learn the letters using their whole body and several sensory systems at the same time.
